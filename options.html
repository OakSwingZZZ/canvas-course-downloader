Student data & grades
What "Student data & grades" includes & how your role affects it
This group is detected automatically by your role. As a teacher/TA it captures everyone's data: Student submissions downloads every student's files, text, comments & rubric scores (all attempts) into a Submissions/ folder — untick it to back up a large course without the submissions — Discussions include every student reply, Grades produces a full Gradebook.csv + Students.csv (with sections) for the whole class, and Quizzes include the question bank with the answer key plus per-student scores. Handle these exports accordingly. As a student the same group gives you only your own view: your submissions, your quiz scores (and answers when the instructor allows it), and your personal grades.

Downloads

How files are saved and how downloads behave.

Before a large export: turn off "Ask where to save each file before downloading" in your browser's download settings. Otherwise the browser prompts you to pick a location for every single file, which is unworkable for a full course. (Chrome/Edge: Settings → Downloads. The ZIP option below also avoids this by producing one file.)
Lower = faster but may overwhelm the browser. Default: 250 ms.
Prepended to the course folder name. Leave empty for no prefix.
On by default. Bundles the whole course into a single .zip instead of hundreds of separate files — so your browser only prompts once, not per file. Much cleaner for your Downloads folder. Very large courses (over ~1.5 GB) fall back to individual files automatically.
When enabled, the extension remembers which files have been downloaded for each course. On subsequent runs, only new or modified files are downloaded. Useful for archiving progressively over a semester. File tracking is stored locally on this device.

Format

Output format for text-based content.

Markdown exports skip styles.css and produce .md files instead of HTML. Best for Obsidian, Notion, or feeding into an LLM. Canvas files (PDFs, etc.) are unaffected.
